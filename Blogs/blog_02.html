The Launch of Twitch and How It Changed the Web

---------------------

Livestreaming is one the most popular forms of entertainment on the web today, be it for news, education, business, social media or for the most popular multi-billion dollar form of media: video games.

History of Twitch

Twitch launched back in 2011 under the development of founder Justin Kan. Justin was studying for a physics and psychology degree at Yale back in 2005 when he founded a start-up called justin.tv, a website where users could create channels and live stream video content called ‘broadcasts’ to other user logged on to watch called ‘viewers’. Over the next 7 years the website would evolve and grow gaining features such as Categories and Headlines where broadcasters could distinguish themselves from others. In 2011 justin.tv separated its ‘Gaming” section into a new website, called Twitch where shortly after all the other sections such as Sports, Music, News and People were moved too.

Some mind-blowing stats from Twitch that might amaze you. (Stats taken January 3rd, 2020)

  • Twitch Has More Than 15 Million Daily Active Users
  • Twitch has between 2.2 and 3.2 Million Broadcasters each month.
  • Over 726 million hours are watched per month, that’s just over 24 million hours a day.
  • Twitch has 75,6% share of all hours watched on all live-broadcasting platforms on the web, that’s including other platforms such as YouTube and Facebook.

Effects on Education

Whether it’s a student learning how to code or someone learning how to cook something new there is a huge variety of learning opportunities that live broadcasting brings. Live broadcasting also plays an important role in delivering distance education for students who may live in inaccessible areas or be very useful in helping students who can’t attend a traditional school. Live broadcasting can also be used to discuss sensitive topics without the uncomfortable person-to-person discussion that many people can’t handle.
