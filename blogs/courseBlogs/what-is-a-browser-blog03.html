What is a Browser? And How does it Work?

---------------------

What is a browser?

A browser (or sometimes referred to a web browser or Internet browser) is a software program used to access or explore pieces of content on the internet. These pieces of content come in all forms such as pictures, videos or documents all displayed on webpages within websites. A browser goes and receives these websites and webpages from online servers and displays them to the user who needs to access the internet.

Short history of the browser

The first browser, called WorldWideWeb, was created by a British computer scientist Sir Tim Berners-Lee at CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research) back in 1989. The most common browsers used today by almost everyone who accesses the internet on a daily basis are ones such as Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome, Apple Safari or Opera.

How does a browser work?

When you type a webpage address (or Uniform Resource Locator (URL)) such as www.google.com into your browser, it is sometimes assumed that the entire webpage is stored on a server somewhere complete and ready to be downloaded and sent back to our browser and then displayed to us. Where in reality you are actually calling up a variety of different requests for different types of content stored in different locations, such as various directories or multiple servers, to be displayed on that webpage. A browser makes a list of some sorts to fetch and load all the small parts of content from the various locations and combines (or renders) them all to display a full webpage. The worlds first website is still online and can be found here.

Hyperlinks and cache

Webpages usually contain hyperlinks to other webpages, where each link contains its or address (URL). When these hyperlinks are clicked (or tapped on a phone), the browser will navigate to this new resource where the process of loading all the content needed for that webpage begins again. Most browser will use an internal cache of webpage resources stored on your computer to help improve loading times when you visit certain webpages . The cache can store large content items such as images or videos so that they do not need to be downloaded from the server again.
