What Vannevar Bush Saw Then

Written by Devan Gray - Friday, February 7, 2020
Edited - Wednesday, June 24, 2020

Link to Article - As We May Think: Vannavader Bush

Pointing out some of the things today around us that Vannevar Bush kind of predicted in a sense.

"Store human writings in a small room with the use of photography and microfilm"

Today writings are photocopied, scanned, or photographed and stored online in folders on servers of computers. Today data is stored on the smallest of devices and is continuously getting smaller compared to the technology of the pass where computers would take up entire rooms and even buildings.

"Speech recording and stenography"

Speech recording with phones – voice messages. The use of Speech Recognition and Natural Language Processing such as Siri on the Apple iPhones or Alexa on the Amazon Echo. Stenography is the quicker process of writing in shorthand and then later transcribing the shorthand on a typewriter. Today there are applications that allow users to scribble something on their phone, tablet or laptop and their scribble is translated to text input. Autocorrect would even be a form of this as the user only has to enter in a short part of a much larger word in order to complete the phase they need to enter.

"Printing immediate"

Today printing is pretty immediate – documents, images, books, or reports can be printed quickly using today high-end printer with some outputting as much as 50 pages per minute. As well as the advancement of being able to print three dimensional objects today.

“The main feature of the memex is the ability to tie two things together at will. In other words, to be able to associate two arbitrary items when wanted. The user is also able to build a trail, in which they name it, insert a name into the code book, and then taps it out on the keyboard. At any time, the user is able to view two items at the same time, parallel viewing. It is also possible to pass items to another memex.”

Sounds and reads exactly like the internet and the use of search engines where any term or item can be inserted into the “search bar”. Google has the ability to search by images. The trail between these items is similar to the hyperlinking between pages on a web browser.

“The trails made can be shared with others and can also be published, like an encyclopaedia. Soon we will be able to establish some kind of direct connection with absorbing material of the record with one of our senses, tactilely, orally, and visually.”

Social media – where almost anything, such as images, locations, videos etc., can be shared with other people who have access to the internet. Most things we seen and access on the internet we are absorbing both visually and audibly with computers and screens, as well as tactilely through touch screens on modern phones and note pads.
