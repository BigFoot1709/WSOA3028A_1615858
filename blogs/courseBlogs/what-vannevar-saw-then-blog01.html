What Vannevar Bush Saw Then

Looking at the 1945 essay by Vannevar Bush and how the technologies he envisioned were almost complete predictions of technological advancements that are around today.

Link to Article - As We May Think: Vannavader Bush

"Store human writings in a small room with the use of photography and microfilm"

Today writings are photocopied, scanned or photographed and stored online in folders on servers of computers.

"Speech recording and stenography"

Speech recording with phones – voice messages. Stenography is the quicker process of writing in shorthand and then later transcribing the shorthand on a typewriter. Today there are applications that allow users to scribble something on their phone, tablet or laptop and their scribble is translated to text input. Autocorrect would even be a form of this as the user only has to enter in a short part of a much larger word in order to complete the phase they need to enter.

"Printing immediate"

Today printing is pretty immediate – documents, images, books or reports can be printed quickly using today high-end printer with some outputting as much as 50 pages per minute.

"The advancement of photography is not going to stop"

The advancement of digital image resolution today is a non-stop train growing moving day to day.
