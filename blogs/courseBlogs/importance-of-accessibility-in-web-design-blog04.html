Importance of Accessibility in Web Designs

Written by Devan Gray - Wednesday, February 26, 2020

The web and internet have become an integral part of almost everyone’s daily lives and both continue to grow more each day. Making sure that a website or webpage can be interacted and experienced by most people if not all does not only allow it to be seen by a much larger audience but will also help in the development of a better and improved web and making the web accessible for everyone.

What is Web Accessibility?

Accessibility means the ability for everyone to have access to something regardless of their condition or circumstance. Wikipedia says, “Accessibility refers to the design of products, devices, services, or environments for people who experience disabilities.”. So, we say that Web Accessibility is a way of designing the web or internet in such a way that makes it accessible for everyone regardless of their circumstance or disabilities. Everyone needs to perceive, experience, understand and interact with the web so that they can, in turn, contribute to the Web just as everyone who has before them – this will only lead to more improved web.

Why Web Accessibility is Important?

The usage of web and internet today is an extremely important part of people’s everyday life be it in education, work, health care, security, banking or entertainment. Therefore, is it important that the web be accessible to everyone to provide equal opportunity to people with disabilities and allow them to participate in everyday society. So, it is always important to have web accessibility in mind with designing and building websites.

A Few Ways of Designing for Web Accessibility

  • The alt-tag (alternative tag), an html attribute used to give a description of an image.
  • Using the html title tag that gives a short description for visitors what is being displayed on the web page.
  • Making sure your website can be navigated by just the use of a keyboard.
