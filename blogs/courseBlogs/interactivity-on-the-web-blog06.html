Interactivity on the Web

What is Interactivity?

Across the variety of fields concerned with interactivity, such as computer science, human-computer interaction, communication, and industrial design there is still some sort of disagreement over the definition of the term interactivity. One thing they all seem to agree on is that there is definitely some sort of relation to the process of the interaction in some way of humans with computers and/or other machines and their user interfaces. Another definition of interactivity from Margaret Rouse on WhatIs.com - In computers, interactivity is the dialog that occurs between a human being (or possibly any other live creature/animal) and a computer program.

Interactivity in Web Design

An interactive web design is the design of a website/webpage that allows for a more enhanced user experience of interaction between the user and the site/page though the user of different inbuilt features and software. Where personalized data is displayed or presented to users that have asked for it or have needed it through the process of said users interacting with the website/webpage.

Benefits of Interactivity

In the field of learning, interactivity strategies utilize a variety of training/teaching software’s that allow employees or students of any type to explore their learning environment and most importantly their field of work at a much more enhanced level. Another benefit of interactivity in learning is that students and employees can learn in their own way and at a pace that is comfortable for them as well as receive almost instance feedback on their progress that allows them to reflect on their own decision making, also allowing trainers/teachers the ability to assess the performance of their learners.

In Interactive Web Design visitors/users have a more personal experience through the ability to customize their data that they see. A benefit of this may see the website gain more visitors over time and thus in turn also enables the website creator to gain more feasible feedback from their users and allows for more improvement. An automated website also saves a lot of time and money when a company’s employees are not around to help visitors/users, instead users have access to information provided at all times.
