Game 5

Ashraful Helps Bangladesh Land Knockout Blow

Written by Steven Stat - Thursday, September 13, 2007

The Warm-Up

The team that set the tournament opener on fire, West Indies, sent home on Thursday afternoon after some good bowling and even better batting from the underdogs Bangladesh as make a chase of 164 look easy as ever.

Bangladesh’s ability to take West Indian wickets at regular intervals definitely saved them from having to chase a much larger total in this afternoon’s game. West Indies being the first team to exit the tournament much have felt they really missed out having played both their games at the wonderful cricket stadium to score huge totals that is Wanderers.

The Match

It all began with Syed Rasel getting the surprised wicket of Chris Gayle out for a duck on just the 3rd ball of the match, a ready shock for the West Indian team it seemed. From there Devon Smith and Shivnarine Chanderpaul seemed to steady the ship at a very slow rate, courtesy of the very economical bowling of Rasel and Abdur Razzak, with a partnership of 95 of 79 balls at a strike rate of just 120. More quick wickets disturbed the flow of the West Indians’ innings. Despite Dwayne Smith’s rapid 29 of just 7 balls to try accelerate his team to something more the West Indies were restricted to 164 for 8 in their 20 overs, a score they must have felt was not enough.

Bangladesh’s innings was made to look easy as Aftab Ahmed and captain Mohammad Ashraful blasted their way though the total with ease never looking like they were in trouble at any stage. Both batsmen manged to keep their team’s run rate above 9 for most of the innings never giving West Indies a chance and winning with 2 whole overs (12 balls) to spare.

Mohammad Ashraful's Innings Highlights


The Stats

  • Mohammad Ashraful scored the fastest fifty in Twenty20 internationals, beating the previous record held by Sanath Jayasuriya, who scored a 50 off 23 balls against New Zealand in Wellington.
  • Ashraful and Aftab Ahmed were involved in a partnership of 109, the fifth instance of a century partnership in Twenty20 internationals. All five hundred -plus stands have come at the Wanderers.
  • Syed Rasel's spell of 4-1-10-1 is the most economical for a bowler having delivered four overs in a Twenty20 international. Rasel's economy rate in Twenty20 matches is second-best behind Mark Gillespie.
  • Shakib Al Hasan's 4 for 34 is the first four-for for a Bangladesh player in a Twenty20 international.
  • Ashraful was at the receiving end while bowling, having conceded 55 runs off his four overs, the second-most runs conceded by a bowler in a Twenty20 international.
